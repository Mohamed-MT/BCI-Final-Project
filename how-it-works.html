How BCIs capture and translate neural activity

Signal acquisition

Implanted microelectrode arrays sit on or in the cortex and detect neuron firing. These raw electrical signals are amplified and filtered. For restoring communication, implanted arrays typically provide the high fidelity needed to decode precise patterns like imagined handwriting or speech movements.

Decoding & machine learning

Machine learning models convert neural patterns into letters, words, or commands. Models are trained per patient because neural signatures vary. A confirmation step (user verifies outputs before sending) reduces the risk of accidental messages.

Output & feedback

Decoded outputs become text, synthetic speech, or device control. Real-time feedback helps users refine their neural signals and increases accuracy over time.

Illustration: system flow

From neural signal → decoder → confirmation → text or speech output. For patients, this means regained communication via thought alone.

Real example

Clinical trials have decoded imagined handwriting or attempted speech from implanted arrays, allowing some patients to produce text at practical speeds. These experiments show the feasibility of returning meaningful communication to people with locked-in conditions.
