Brain–Computer Interfaces
Introduction Applications Critical Discussion Recommendations

Social implications

  • BCIs offer increased independence and communication abilities for individuals with disabilities.
  • They raise concerns about who controls neural data and how this information may be used or misused.

Risks and accountability

  • Invasive BCIs involve surgical risks, device failures, emotional strain, and potential unintended outputs.
  • These issues create uncertainty about whether responsibility lies with users, clinicians, or developers.

Ethical lenses

  • Utilitarian perspective: BCIs are ethical if the benefits such as restored communication and improved quality of life outweigh potential harms.
  • Deontological perspective: Emphasizes autonomy, informed consent, and strict protections for neural data regardless of overall societal benefit.
