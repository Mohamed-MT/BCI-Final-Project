Brain–Computer Interfaces
Introduction Applications Critical Discussion Recommendations

What is a Brain–Computer Interface (BCI)?

  • BCIs enable direct communication between the brain and external devices by translating electrical neural activity into digital commands.
  • This allows individuals to control computers, robotic limbs, and communication tools without physical movement.

Types and significance

  • BCIs can be non-invasive (external sensors) or invasive (implants placed directly on or inside the brain), offering different accuracy levels.
  • The technology is rapidly transitioning from research environments into real-world use for people with severe disabilities.

Promise and ethical concerns

  • BCIs have the potential to restore independence and communication for people who cannot move or speak.
  • They also introduce major ethical concerns involving privacy, autonomy, safety, and the implications of direct neural interfacing.
