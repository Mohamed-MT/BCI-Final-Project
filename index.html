What is a Brain–Computer Interface (BCI) — and why it matters

BCIs are systems that read neural activity and translate it into actions for a computer or device. They range from non-invasive headsets to implanted microelectrode arrays. We focus on using implanted BCIs to restore communication for people with severe paralysis because that application has immediate, measurable human benefit while also raising urgent ethical and safety questions.

Why this use case

Restoring speech and communication to people who cannot speak returns autonomy, improves mental health, and enables decision-making. That makes it one of the highest-impact, ethically defensible uses of BCI technology when done safely and with respect for patients’ rights.
