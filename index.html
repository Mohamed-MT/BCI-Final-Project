Brain–Computer Interfaces
Introduction Applications Critical Discussion Recommendations

What is a Brain–Computer Interface (BCI)?

  • BCIs enable direct communication between the human brain and external devices by translating electrical brain signals into digital commands.
  • This allows users to control technologies such as computers, robotic limbs, and communication tools without physical movement.

Types and significance

  • BCIs can be non-invasive (external sensors) or invasive (implants), with implanted devices offering more accurate signal detection.
  • The technology is rapidly moving from research settings into real-world use for individuals with severe disabilities.

Promise and ethical concerns

  • BCIs have potential to restore communication and independence for people who cannot move or speak.
  • They also raise ethical concerns involving privacy, safety, autonomy, and the impact of technology that directly interfaces with neural activity.
