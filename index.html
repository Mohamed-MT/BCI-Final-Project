BCI to Restore Communication

We study implanted Brain–Computer Interfaces (BCIs) used to restore communication for people with severe paralysis. These systems translate neural activity into text or synthesized speech, returning independence and enabling meaningful connection. Our focus is narrow and practical: medical BCIs for communication.

Why we focus on communication

Restoring the ability to communicate has immediate patient impact. It is an ethically strong use case because it addresses fundamental autonomy: decisions, preferences, and relationships depend on communication.

Quick facts

Implant type: intracortical microelectrode arrays
Primary stakeholder: medical centers & clinicians
Ethical lenses: utilitarian & deontology
